Metamason is a startup that is creating customized CPAP masks for people suffering from sleep apnea. We do this by scanning the patient's face, uploading that scan to our servers, and using a proprietary process to turn the scan into a mask.

As one of the first three developers working at Metamason, I am responsible for creating the software that is integral to the success of the company. We are running a complicated technology stack, using React and Electron to build for both web applications and a Windows application. Our Windows application uses the RealSense 3D camera to allow clinicians to scan patients. Our API server runs Ruby on Rails utilizing JSON API and we're using Node.js to process scans and turn them into the manufacturing files to print mask molds.

HigherEd Web Application

Calculus Education
THREE.js
Javascript
Ember.js
Node.js
3D Printing

HigherEd is a project I worked on all throughout my senior year of school. The original idea was to create a startup that would help teachers create digitally integrated lessons. I had noticed through my years as a tutor that students would generally learn better with visual aids, and so the idea was to have lessons that would teach a concept, and then show a visual demo. After talking with teachers and students and receiving feedback, I decided to focus on creating an application that would take virtual graphical visuals and then create .stl files that could then be 3D printed. My short term goal was to create a bridge building application, that would allow students to virtually design a bridge, simulate weighted loads on it, and then 3D print it and test it in the real world. Unfortunately, I ran out of time before I was able to get the simulation working, but I was able to create a rough application to allow someone to design and print out a bridge.

Although I'm still working on updating the application to newer versions and dependencies to demonstrate the bridge building functionality. The Ember application is embedded within this website, where you can play around with Calculus concepts like revolutions of solids, integrals, derivatives, and more.

Orbital Transfer Vehicle Design

Aero Engineering
Thermal Analysis

The Aerospace senior design capstone is a year long class that takes you from the mission objectives through concept, development, multiple design reviews, and ultimately to a final presentation/paper. This is usually part of the American Institute of Aeronautics and Astronautics' design competition. The competition for our year was to create an Orbital Transfer Vehicle capable of transporting 50,000 lbs from Low Earth Orbit to Earth-Moon Lagrange Points 1 and 2 and 15,000 lbs back. Each transfer had to be within 6 days, and the vehicle had to be reusable for at least 10 missions.

I was the Power and Thermal Engineer, so my responsibilities included keeping the spacecraft powered at all times, and the internal electronics at the correct temperatures. I picked solar arrays and batteries and sized them appropriately, designed a main bus loosely based off of the space station and other similarly sized spacecraft, ran thermal simulations to determine the correct application of reflective and absorptive materials, all while coordinating with 6 other engineers to ensure that the vehicle integrated each section seamlessly. As a result, our team placed first in the AIAA competition, winning the national award, and I had the opportunity to present our design at the annual AIAA conference.
